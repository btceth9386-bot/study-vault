# Idiom: Rule of Thumb

- **One-sentence definition**: A general guideline based on experience — not an exact rule, but a practical principle that usually works.
- **Why it exists / what problem it solves**: English speakers constantly use this phrase to share practical wisdom without sounding dogmatic. It signals "this is a useful guideline, not an absolute law." If you don't know it, you'll miss the nuance in professional conversations.
- **Keywords**: rule of thumb, idiom, guideline, principle, general rule

## Summary

"Rule of thumb" means a practical guideline that works most of the time. It's not a strict rule — it's wisdom from experience. You'll hear it in every professional context: business, cooking, medicine, construction, software.

The key thing to understand: when someone says "the rule of thumb is...", they're giving you a shortcut that's usually right but has exceptions. It's softer than saying "the rule is..." — it leaves room for judgment.

**Useful sentence frames:**
- "The rule of thumb is [guideline]."
- "As a rule of thumb, [advice]."
- "The [profession]'s rule of thumb: [principle]."

## Example

From the lesson:
> "Remember the carpenter's rule of thumb: measure twice, cut once."

More examples from the lesson's practice section:
> "The rule of thumb in making a sale is confidence."
> "The tailor's rule of thumb is twice the circumference of the wrist is the circumference of the neck."
> "You don't have to take everything I say about marketing as a rule of thumb." (= don't treat it as a universal guideline)
