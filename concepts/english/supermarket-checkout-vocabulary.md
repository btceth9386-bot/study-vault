# Supermarket Checkout Vocabulary

- **One-sentence definition**: The English words and phrases you need to navigate paying for groceries at a US supermarket — from getting in line to getting your receipt.
- **Why it exists / what problem it solves**: Grocery shopping is one of the first real-world situations where you need functional English. If you don't know these words, a simple checkout becomes stressful. These phrases are also used metaphorically ("express lane to success", "your patience has expired").
- **Keywords**: express checkout lane, belt, shopping cart, coupons, expire, club card, debit, credit, cash back, receipt, paper or plastic

## Summary

A US supermarket checkout has a specific vocabulary that you'll encounter every single time you shop. Here's the flow:

1. You push your **shopping cart** to the **checkout lane**. The **express checkout lane** is for 15 items or fewer (faster).
2. You place items on the **belt** (conveyor belt that moves items to the cashier).
3. The cashier asks "**Paper or plastic?**" — meaning paper bags or plastic bags.
4. You might hand over **coupons** (discount vouchers). Watch out — they can **expire** (become invalid after a date).
5. Payment: **club card** (store card), **debit or credit**, and optionally **cash back** (extra charge on your card, cashier gives you cash — like a mini ATM).
6. You get your **receipt** (paper proof of purchase) and leave.

The verb **expire** is especially useful beyond supermarkets: credit cards expire, driver's licenses expire, milk expires, passports expire.

Three small phrases are worth treating as fixed checkout patterns:

- **Express checkout lane** means the faster line for shoppers with a limited number of items, such as "15 items or fewer." Use **fewer** because items are countable.
- **Paper or plastic?** is a shortened question meaning "Do you want paper bags or plastic bags?"
- **Cash back** at checkout is not a discount. It means the cashier charges extra to your debit transaction and gives you that extra amount as physical cash.
- **Payment method questions** are short: "Will this be debit or credit?", "Can I pay by card?", and "Can I pay with cash?"
- **Tap your card** means hold the card near the payment machine. **Insert your card** means put the card into the machine.
- **Receipt questions** close the transaction: "Would you like a receipt?", "Do you need the receipt?", or "Can I have the receipt, please?"

## Example

Full checkout dialogue:
> Cashier: "This is the express checkout lane for 15 items or fewer."
> Customer: "I have 16 items. Cut me some slack!"
> Cashier: "Place your items on the belt. Paper or plastic?"
> Customer: "Plastic. I also have coupons."
> Cashier: "These coupons expired yesterday."
> Customer: "Darn. It's just not my day."
> Cashier: "Club card or cash? Debit or credit? Cash back?"
> Customer: "Debit. $50 cash back please."
> Cashier: "Your total is $78.33. Here's your receipt."

Card-payment practice:
> Customer: "Can I pay by card?"
> Cashier: "Yes, of course. Please tap your card."
> Cashier: "Payment approved. Would you like a receipt?"
> Customer: "Yes, please. Can I have the receipt?"
