# Construction Tools Vocabulary

- **One-sentence definition**: Common English words for construction tools — where the tool's name usually tells you what it does.
- **Why it exists / what problem it solves**: These words appear everywhere in English — hardware stores, home improvement shows, and metaphors ("nailed it", "drilling down into the data"). Knowing them unlocks both literal and figurative conversations.
- **Keywords**: hammer, nail gun, circular saw, drill, sander, level, measuring tape, 2x4, splinters

## Summary

English construction tool names follow a helpful pattern: the name describes the function. A **nail gun** is a gun that shoots nails. A **circular saw** is a saw with a circular blade. A **measuring tape** is a tape for measuring. Once you see this compound-noun pattern, new tool names become self-explanatory.

Key tools from the lesson:
- **Hammer** → hits nails into wood (basic, everyone knows this)
- **Nail gun** → uses compressed air to shoot nails (faster than a hammer)
- **Circular saw** → power saw with a round spinning blade for cutting wood
- **Drill** → makes holes in wood/metal (also a verb: "drill some holes")
- **Sander** → machine with sandpaper that makes wood smooth (verb: "to sand")
- **Level** → tool with water/bubble inside that shows if something is perfectly flat
- **Measuring tape** → flexible ruler that rolls up, fits in your pocket

Key materials: **2x4** (two-by-four) = the most common lumber size (2 inches × 4 inches). **Splinters** = tiny sharp pieces of wood that stick out and can get under your skin.

## Example

Dialogue from the lesson:
> "The nails won't go in. The wood is too hard. I think I'm going to need the nail gun."
> "Help me cut this 2x4. Pass me that circular saw."
> "I think you should sand the edges. Look at all these splinters."

Notice the pattern: "Pass me the [tool]" is the standard way to ask for a tool in English.
