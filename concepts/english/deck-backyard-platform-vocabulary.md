# Deck as Backyard Platform Vocabulary

- **One-sentence definition**: In home and construction English, a **deck** is an elevated outdoor platform, usually made of wood, attached to or built behind a house.
- **Why it exists / what problem it solves**: Learners may know "deck" from playing cards or ships, but North American home English often uses it for an outdoor living space. Knowing this meaning helps with home-improvement, real-estate, and construction conversations.
- **Keywords**: deck, backyard, platform, wooden platform, patio, home improvement

## Summary

A deck is like an outdoor room without walls. It is a raised platform, often behind a house, where people put chairs, a table, or a grill. In the lesson, the workers are hired to build a deck and turn a plain lawn into a better outdoor space.

The word matters because "deck" has several meanings in English. In this construction context, it is not a set of cards and not part of a ship. It is the thing being built.

## Example

> "We've been hired to build a deck on this house and turn this boring lawn into a backyard oasis."

In everyday English:

> "We're eating dinner on the deck tonight."
> "The old deck needs new boards."
