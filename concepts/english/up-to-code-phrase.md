# Up to Code Phrase

- **One-sentence definition**: **Up to code** means meeting the official rules, safety standards, or legal requirements for a building or system.
- **Why it exists / what problem it solves**: In construction and inspection, good-looking work is not enough. Work must meet external standards so it is safe, legal, and acceptable to inspectors.
- **Keywords**: up to code, standards, inspection, safety, compliance, requirements

## Summary

"Up to code" means the work passes the required standard. A deck can look nice but still fail inspection if it is not built safely. In the lesson, the workers are told to follow the plans exactly because the home inspectors are thorough.

The phrase is most literal in construction, electrical work, plumbing, and safety. It can also be used more generally to mean "meets the required standard," but its strongest meaning is about official rules.

## Example

> "We really need to make sure that everything is up to code. The home inspectors here are pretty thorough."

Other examples:

> "The wiring is not up to code."
> "Before we sell the house, we need to make sure the deck is up to code."
