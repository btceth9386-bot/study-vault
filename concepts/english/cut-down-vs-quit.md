# Cut Down vs Quit

- **一句話定義**："Cut down" means to reduce the amount or frequency of something, while "quit" means to stop doing it completely.
- **為什麼存在 / 解決什麼問題**：The distinction prevents a common misunderstanding in habit-change conversations. Someone who says they will "cut down on smoking" is not promising zero cigarettes; they are promising fewer cigarettes. This pattern also transfers to food, workload, salt, alcohol, screen time, and other repeated behaviors.

## 摘要

Cut Down vs Quit is a practical English pattern you can recognize and reuse in real conversations. "Cut down" means to reduce the amount or frequency of something, while "quit" means to stop doing it completely. The important point is not only the dictionary meaning, but when a speaker would naturally choose it. The distinction prevents a common misunderstanding in habit-change conversations.
