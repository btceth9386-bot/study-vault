# Idiom: Give 110 Percent

- **One-sentence definition**: To **give 110 percent** means to try your absolute hardest and put in more effort than normally expected.
- **Why it exists / what problem it solves**: This idiom is common in work and sports settings when someone wants to motivate a team under pressure. If learners read it literally, the math looks impossible; the real meaning is maximum effort.
- **Keywords**: give 110 percent, effort, motivation, teamwork, urgency, workplace

## Summary

"Give 110 percent" is a motivational phrase. It does not mean there is a real number above 100 percent. It means "try very hard," "give everything you have," or "go beyond the normal effort."

In the lesson, the contractor says this because the team has only one day to finish the deck. The phrase fits moments with urgency, deadlines, competition, or a difficult shared goal.

## Example

> "We really have to give 110 percent today if we want to finish this project on time."

Other examples:

> "The team gave 110 percent in the final quarter."
> "If you want to pass the exam, you need to give 110 percent this week."
