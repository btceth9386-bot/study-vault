# Highlights

- `Vocabulary Preview: spew`: "Spew" is introduced as forcefully flowing or shooting out, used for steam, lava, or someone vomiting.
- `Vocabulary Preview: busted`: "Busted" is taught as informal English for broken or not working.
- `Dialogue: car trouble`: The driver says the car started spewing white smoke and "died on me", giving learners a natural way to report a breakdown.
- `Dialogue: pop the hood`: The mechanic asks the driver to pop the hood so they can look at the engine area.
- `Language Takeaway: radiator`: The white smoke is explained as steam from the radiator after the engine overheated.
- `Language Takeaway: pistons and transmission`: The mechanic identifies busted pistons and a busted transmission, introducing major engine and drivetrain vocabulary.
- `Language Takeaway: spare parts and fuel pump`: Repair work requires ordering replacement parts and working with the electrical system, fuel pump, and engine.
- `Fluency Builder: die on`: The phrase means a device or vehicle stops working while someone is using it.
- `Fluency Builder: take apart`: The lesson contrasts taking parts out with putting everything back together again.
- `Extra practice segment`: The simple mechanic practice reinforces practical repair-shop questions such as "What seems to be the problem?", "How much does it cost?", and "How long will it take?"
