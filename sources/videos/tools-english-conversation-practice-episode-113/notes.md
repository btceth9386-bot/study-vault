# Tools | English Conversation Practice | Episode 113

## Summary

An EnglishPod lesson where hosts Marco and Erica teach construction vocabulary and workplace idioms through a dialogue about a team building a deck in one day. The lesson starts by defining a **deck** as an elevated wooden platform behind a house and **plans** as the construction map that shows how something should be built. It then moves through tool names such as nail gun, circular saw, sander, level, and measuring tape, along with job-site words like 2x4, bolts, splinters, and drill.

What makes the source useful is that it mixes concrete nouns with transferable work language. The team leader says the crew must **give 110%**, reminds them that everything must be **up to code**, and repeats the carpenter's **rule of thumb**: measure twice, cut once. Another worker tells a teammate to **take pride in your work** after noticing unsafe splinters. Because of that mix, the lesson works both as a construction English lesson and as a general workplace English lesson about effort, quality, safety, and following plans under time pressure.

## Knowledge Map

- Construction tool vocabulary: hammer, nail gun, circular saw, sander, level, measuring tape, drill
- Construction materials/terms: deck, 2x4, splinters, bolts, nails, screws, wood
- Workplace idioms: "give 110%", "rule of thumb", "take pride in your work"
- Construction phrases: "up to code", "measure twice cut once", "pass me that...", "follow the plans exactly"
- Dialogue context: contractor giving instructions to a team under time pressure

## Key Takeaways

- A **deck** is an elevated wooden platform behind a house, used for outdoor dining/barbecue
- **Plans** are the construction map or design that shows how the project should be built
- A **2x4** (two-by-four) is the most common lumber size in construction: 2 inches × 4 inches
- **Rule of thumb** = a general principle or guideline (not a strict rule), usable in any context
- **Give 110%** = try your absolute hardest, give more than what's expected
- **Take pride in your work** = work carefully and well so you can feel good about the result
- **Up to code** = meeting official building/safety standards
- **Measure twice, cut once** = the carpenter's rule of thumb — double-check before acting (applicable to any careful work)
- Tool vocabulary pattern: the tool name often describes its function (nail gun = gun for nails, circular saw = saw with circular blade, measuring tape = tape for measuring)
