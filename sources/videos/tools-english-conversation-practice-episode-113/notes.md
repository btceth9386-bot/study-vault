# Tools | English Conversation Practice | Episode 113

## Summary

An EnglishPod lesson where hosts Marco and Erica teach construction/tool vocabulary and workplace idioms through a dialogue about a team building a deck (elevated wooden platform) in one day. The lesson covers tool names (nail gun, circular saw, sander, level, measuring tape), construction terms (2x4, splinters, bolts, drill), and three fluency phrases useful in any work context. The dialogue demonstrates natural conversational English in a workplace setting with time pressure.

## Knowledge Map

- Construction tool vocabulary: hammer, nail gun, circular saw, sander, level, measuring tape, drill
- Construction materials/terms: deck, 2x4, splinters, bolts, nails, screws, wood
- Workplace idioms: "give 110%", "rule of thumb", "take pride in your work"
- Construction phrases: "up to code", "measure twice cut once", "pass me that..."
- Dialogue context: contractor giving instructions to a team under time pressure

## Key Takeaways

- A **deck** is an elevated wooden platform behind a house, used for outdoor dining/barbecue
- A **2x4** (two-by-four) is the most common lumber size in construction: 2 inches × 4 inches
- **Rule of thumb** = a general principle or guideline (not a strict rule), usable in any context
- **Give 110%** = try your absolute hardest, give more than what's expected
- **Take pride in your work** = work carefully and well so you can feel good about the result
- **Up to code** = meeting official building/safety standards
- **Measure twice, cut once** = the carpenter's rule of thumb — double-check before acting (applicable to any careful work)
- Tool vocabulary pattern: the tool name often describes its function (nail gun = gun for nails, circular saw = saw with circular blade, measuring tape = tape for measuring)
