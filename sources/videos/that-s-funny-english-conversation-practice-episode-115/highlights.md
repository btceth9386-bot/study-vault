# Highlights

- `Opening / topic setup`: The lesson frames jokes as part of English-speaking culture, not just entertainment vocabulary.
- `Vocabulary preview: moo and dam/damn`: The hosts pre-teach sounds and homophones needed to understand later punchlines.
- `Vocabulary preview: nostrils`: "Nostrils" is introduced as the two holes in the nose, enabling the final joke about gorillas.
- `Dialogue: open mic night`: The announcer introduces an open mic night, comics, and a stand-up performer, establishing the performance setting.
- `Dialogue: routine and zingers`: The comic says he has worked on his routine and has "zingers", showing vocabulary for prepared jokes and punchy one-liners.
- `Dialogue: tough crowd and hecklers`: Audience rejection is described through "tough crowd" and "hecklers", useful for describing hostile or unresponsive audiences.
- `Fluency Builder: movies/moo-vies`: The cow joke depends on sound-based wordplay, where "movies" is pronounced to echo "moo".
- `Fluency Builder: dam/damn`: The fish joke depends on a homophone: a dam is a wall in water, while "damn" is an interjection.
- `Closing: clean jokes`: The hosts ask listeners to share jokes but say they should be clean, introducing register awareness for humor.
