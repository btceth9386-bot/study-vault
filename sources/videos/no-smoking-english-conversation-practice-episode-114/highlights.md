# Highlights

- `Opening / vocabulary preview`: "Pack of smokes" is introduced as informal speech for a pack of cigarettes, useful for recognizing casual smoker vocabulary.
- `Dialogue: conflict about quitting`: The smoker distinguishes "cut down" from "quit", which frames the main lesson around reducing a habit versus stopping it completely.
- `Language Takeaway: ashtray`: "It smells like an ashtray in here" shows how a concrete noun can become a complaint about smell and environment.
- `Language Takeaway: light up`: "You can't just light up like before" teaches "light up" as a phrasal verb meaning to start smoking.
- `Language Takeaway: willpower and urge`: The speaker says she lacks the willpower to fight the urge to reach for cigarettes, pairing self-control language with habit-craving language.
- `Fluency Builder: cold turkey`: "Go cold turkey overnight" is explained as stopping a habit immediately and completely, especially smoking.
- `Fluency Builder: crack down`: Smoking laws are described as "cracking down", meaning authorities are becoming stricter about a behavior.
- `Discussion: social vs habitual smoker`: The hosts contrast occasional social smoking with habitual smoking, which helps learners describe frequency and lifestyle.
- `Extra practice segment`: The repeated no-smoking practice gives polite sentence frames: "This is a no smoking area", "Please smoke outside", and "Smoking is not allowed here."
