- **一句話定義**："Go cold turkey" means to stop a habit suddenly and completely, without gradually reducing it.
- **為什麼存在**：This idiom appears often in conversations about smoking, alcohol, sugar, social media, and other habits. It gives a concise way to say that someone stopped all at once instead of slowly cutting down, and it often implies that the change is difficult.
- **與既有概念的關聯**：Related to `cut-down-vs-quit` because both describe habit-change strategies. Related to existing idiom concepts such as `rule-of-thumb-idiom` and `cut-me-some-slack-idiom`, but it is not a merge candidate because the meaning and use case are different.
