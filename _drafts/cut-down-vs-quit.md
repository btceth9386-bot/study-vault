- **一句話定義**："Cut down" means to reduce the amount or frequency of something, while "quit" means to stop doing it completely.
- **為什麼存在**：The distinction prevents a common misunderstanding in habit-change conversations. Someone who says they will "cut down on smoking" is not promising zero cigarettes; they are promising fewer cigarettes. This pattern also transfers to food, workload, salt, alcohol, screen time, and other repeated behaviors.
- **與既有概念的關聯**：Related to `expire-everyday-validity-verb` as another everyday verb pattern where the learner must understand practical usage rather than just dictionary meaning. It is also related to `smoking-cessation-vocabulary`, but deserves its own draft because the contrast is broadly reusable outside smoking.
